# Node Types
NetworkNode: Physical or logical network entity (cell, gNB, UE).
ProtocolMessage: Message for network operations.
Timer: Time-based event triggering actions.
Procedure: Sequence of network operations or steps.
UserState: Current state or mode of a user/device.
Feature: High-level functional capability or service enhancement (e.g., Network Slicing, URLLC).

# Relationships (Allowed Verbs)
SENDS, RECEIVES, TRIGGERED_BY, TRANSITIONS_TO, CONTAINS, EXPIRES_DURING, HAS_VALUE, CONFIGURES, HAS_CONFIGURATIONS, REQUIRES, STARTS, STOPS

# Semantic Rules
- NetworkNodes SEND and RECEIVE ProtocolMessages.
- ProtocolMessages can be TRIGGERED_BY Timers.
- Procedures can TRANSITION_TO UserStates or other Procedures.
- Information elements and containers often CONTAINS messages or other elements.
- Timers can be STARTED or STOPPED by Procedures or Messages.
- Configurations CONFIGURE network behaviors or cells.

# Domain Knowledge
UE and gNB are examples of NetworkNode.
RRCSetup and RRCResume are examples of ProtocolMessage.
T300 and T301 are examples of Timer.
RRC Connection Establishment is a Procedure.
RRC_CONNECTED and RRC_IDLE are UserStates.
